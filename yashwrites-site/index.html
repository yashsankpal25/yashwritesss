The Thing About Silence

Harsh is twenty-one and occupies every room without filling it. Moni is 1,807 kilometres away, studying medicine beside a sea she photographs thirty different ways. When a follow request reconnects them after years, what begins as catching up becomes something else entirely — a year of voice notes and blue ticks and everything that lives in the space between what you feel and what you manage to say.

The Author

Yash writes about the things we feel but never say aloud

Yash is a contemporary Indian author whose fiction lives in the precise gap between what people feel and what they manage to say. His work is drawn from the specific textures of modern connection — the blue ticks, the voice notes, the 1,807-kilometre silences — and gives language to experiences that most people carry without finding words for.

His debut novel follows Harsh across a year of unrequited love and daily voice notes, and has been described by readers as "the most honest account of being twenty-one in the digital age" — not because it flatters the experience, but because it refuses to soften it.

He is currently writing his second novel. He believes the right sentence, said precisely, does more than a hundred approximate ones.
